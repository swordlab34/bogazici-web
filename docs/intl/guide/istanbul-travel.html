How many days in Istanbul for dental treatment?

Last updated · 9 July 2026

If you are planning dental treatment abroad, one question comes first: how long will I need to be in Istanbul? The honest answer is that it depends on the treatment, and this guide sets out realistic timeframes for the most common ones, along with how the visits are usually planned, what recovery to expect and what to arrange before you travel. Your personal schedule is confirmed by your dentist after examination and planned with your coordinator.

Why the length of stay varies

There is no single answer to "how many days", because dental treatments differ in nature. Some, such as veneers or crowns, are mostly a matter of preparation and fitting and can be completed in a single, shorter stay. Others, such as implants, include a biological healing period during which the implant fuses with the bone; this cannot be rushed and is usually spread across two visits. Understanding this difference is the key to planning your trip realistically.

Typical timeframes by treatment

The following are general guides; your exact schedule is set after examination.

  • Veneers and crowns: often a single visit of around five to seven days, covering preparation, digital design and fitting of the finished restorations.
  • Implants: usually two visits. A first of about three to five days for examination, 3D imaging and placement; then two to six months of healing at home; then a second visit of about five to seven days to fit the permanent teeth.
  • Full-arch (All-on-4 / All-on-6): planned over two visits, with a fixed temporary set of teeth often fitted early and the final teeth completed after healing.
  • Root canal and general treatment: frequently completed within a single short visit, depending on the tooth.

Because our laboratory is in the same building as the clinic, restorations are made and adjusted on site, which helps keep the fitting stage within a single trip rather than sending work out and waiting.

Made where you are treated

Your teeth are designed and made under the same roof where you are treated, so adjustments take minutes, not another trip.

How a two-visit plan usually works

For treatments that need healing, the two-visit model is the norm and it works around your life rather than against it. On the first visit, the groundwork is done: examination, imaging, any surgery and, where possible, temporary teeth. You then return home for the healing period and continue your normal routine. On the second visit, the permanent teeth are completed. Your coordinator plans both trips with you in advance, so the timing of flights and stays is clear from the start. Between visits, your dentist stays reachable for any questions.

What to plan before you travel

A little preparation makes the trip smoother:

  • Share records early: any recent X-rays or reports help with initial planning before you arrive.
  • Allow a buffer: keep a spare day at the end of a visit in case a review or minor adjustment is helpful.
  • Plan light activity: after surgery the first day or two are best kept calm; sightseeing can wait until you feel comfortable.
  • Ask about your schedule: confirm the expected number of appointments and days so accommodation and flights match.

Your coordinator can help align the treatment schedule with travel and accommodation so the visit is predictable.

Recovery and comfort during your stay

What recovery looks like depends on the treatment. After implant surgery, the first days may bring mild swelling and tenderness, usually well managed with prescribed medication and soft food. Veneers and crowns generally involve little recovery beyond brief sensitivity. In all cases it is sensible to keep the first day after any surgery calm and to follow your dentist's aftercare advice. If you are combining treatment with seeing the city, planning the more active days toward the end of your stay tends to work best.

Care after you return home

Treatment does not end when you fly home. For implants, the healing months at home are part of the process, and good daily care protects the result: brushing twice a day, cleaning between teeth and regular check-ups, wherever you are. Your dentist reviews your treatment at each visit and stays in touch between them. Keeping up your routine care, as set out in our preventive care guide, is what makes the result last.

Questions patients ask

Can I have implants in a single trip?

The placement surgery is usually one visit, but implants need a healing period of two to six months before the permanent teeth are fitted, so treatment is normally planned over two trips. In selected cases a fixed temporary can be fitted early; your dentist advises after examination.

How many days do veneers take?

Veneers are often completed within a single visit of around five to seven days, covering preparation, digital design and fitting. Because our laboratory is on site, the making and adjustment stage stays within the same trip.

Can I see the city while I am here for treatment?

Often yes, with sensible planning. It is best to keep the first day or two after any surgery calm, and to plan more active days toward the end of your stay. Your schedule is planned so appointments and free time fit together.

What should I arrange before I travel?

Sharing any recent X-rays or reports helps with early planning, and confirming the expected number of appointments lets you match flights and accommodation. Your coordinator helps align the treatment schedule with your travel.

What happens if I need an adjustment after I go home?

Your dentist stays reachable between visits, and any review is planned into your schedule. Keeping a spare day at the end of a visit also allows for a minor adjustment if it is helpful.
